# Supply Chain (harness-105)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
The Supply Chain harness optimizes the flow of materials, products, and services from suppliers through operations to customers. It encompasses rigorous supplier evaluation using standardized assessment frameworks, systematic procurement process design balancing cost and quality, inventory optimization balancing holding costs against stockout risks, comprehensive logistics planning for cost-effective delivery, and proactive risk mitigation strategies addressing supply disruptions and vulnerabilities.

## Expert Roles

**Supplier Evaluation Manager**
- Develops supplier qualification criteria and assessment frameworks
- Conducts vendor risk assessment and financial stability analysis
- Performs quality audits and compliance verification
- Benchmarks supplier performance against industry standards
- Maintains supplier scorecard and performance tracking systems

**Procurement Process Designer**
- Designs end-to-end procurement workflows and approval hierarchies
- Develops RFQ and vendor comparison methodologies
- Creates contract templates and negotiation frameworks
- Establishes supplier relationship management protocols
- Implements cost reduction initiatives and spend analysis

**Inventory Optimization Specialist**
- Analyzes demand patterns and forecasting accuracy
- Calculates optimal inventory levels balancing carrying and shortage costs
- Designs inventory classification systems (ABC analysis)
- Recommends safety stock and reorder point policies
- Implements inventory management systems and tracking procedures

**Logistics & Distribution Planner**
- Designs distribution network configuration and warehouse locations
- Evaluates logistics providers and negotiates service levels
- Optimizes shipping routes and consolidation strategies
- Implements tracking systems and visibility infrastructure
- Plans for seasonal demand fluctuations and peak periods

**Supply Chain Risk Officer**
- Identifies supply chain vulnerabilities and single points of failure
- Develops supplier diversification and dual-sourcing strategies
- Creates business continuity and contingency plans
- Establishes risk monitoring and early warning systems
- Plans for natural disasters, geopolitical, and market disruptions

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Map current supply chain and identify critical materials
- Document existing suppliers, contracts, and performance data
- Assess current inventory levels, turnover, and costs
- Identify supply chain pain points and opportunities
- Clarify business objectives and risk tolerance

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Create supplier evaluation scorecard and conduct assessments
- Design procurement process with approval workflows
- Calculate optimal inventory levels and reorder policies
- Analyze logistics network and recommend improvements
- Develop risk register with mitigation and contingency plans

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Present supplier recommendations with business case
- Validate procurement process with stakeholders
- Implement inventory systems and monitoring dashboards
- Establish logistics performance metrics and reviews
- Create risk monitoring protocols and response playbooks

## Deliverables
- Supplier Evaluation Report (assessment scorecard, recommendations, risk profiles)
- Procurement Process Design (workflows, templates, approval authorities)
- Inventory Optimization Plan (demand forecast, safety stock levels, reorder policies)
- Logistics Network Analysis (warehouse locations, carrier selection, cost optimization)
- Supply Chain Risk Plan (vulnerability assessment, diversification strategy, contingency plans)

## Extension Skills
- **Quality Assurance Manager** — Implements supplier quality monitoring and improvement programs
- **Sustainability & Ethics Auditor** — Evaluates supplier environmental and labor practices
- **Demand Planning Specialist** — Develops advanced forecasting models and demand management

## Error Handling

**Inadequate Supplier Data** — Request historical performance records and audits. Conduct on-site visits and reference checks. Use provisional assessment while gathering complete data.

**Inventory Optimization Resistance** — Demonstrate cost savings with historical data. Address concerns about stockouts through safety stock analysis. Implement gradually with ongoing monitoring.

**Logistics Cost Overruns** — Benchmark against industry rates. Consolidate shipments to reduce per-unit costs. Renegotiate carrier contracts based on volume commitments.

**Supply Disruptions Discovered Too Late** — Establish real-time tracking and alert systems. Create risk monitoring dashboards with automatic notifications. Increase supplier communication frequency.

**Conflicting Objectives** — Clearly articulate trade-offs between cost, quality, speed, and risk. Create decision frameworks and approval processes. Monitor performance against multiple balanced metrics.

**Single Supplier Dependencies** — Identify critical materials with single sources. Develop dual-sourcing strategy with transition timeline. Establish supplier development programs for backup suppliers.
