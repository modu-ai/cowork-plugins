# ESG Reporting (harness-100-esg)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
ESG Reporting is a structured framework for creating comprehensive environmental, social, and governance reports that communicate organizational sustainability performance to investors, regulators, and stakeholders. This harness guides non-technical users through materiality assessment, framework alignment (GRI, SASB), stakeholder engagement planning, and metric tracking to produce credible, actionable sustainability narratives.

## Expert Roles

**ESG Strategy Director**
- Lead materiality assessment workshops and identify priority ESG topics
- Map organizational capabilities against reporting frameworks (GRI, SASB, TCFD)
- Define reporting scope, boundaries, and stakeholder groups
- Establish ESG KPI targets and multi-year improvement roadmaps
- Champion cross-functional governance structures

**Sustainability Data Manager**
- Consolidate environmental data (emissions, energy, water, waste)
- Aggregate social metrics (diversity, safety, employee engagement, community impact)
- Verify governance indicators (board composition, compliance, ethics training)
- Manage data quality and audit trails for third-party verification
- Create centralized dashboard for real-time metric tracking

**Stakeholder Engagement Lead**
- Design and execute stakeholder consultation processes (surveys, interviews, forums)
- Document stakeholder expectations and material topic prioritization
- Build relationships with investors, NGOs, regulatory bodies, and community representatives
- Communicate reporting roadmap and respond to stakeholder feedback
- Manage transparency in disclosure practices

**Report Narrative Writer**
- Develop compelling ESG narratives linking strategy to performance
- Create executive summary, themes, and case studies from raw data
- Ensure alignment with industry standards and comparative benchmarking
- Write forward-looking statements on sustainability commitments
- Integrate feedback from leadership and external reviewers

**Third-Party Verification Coordinator**
- Prepare documentation packages for external auditors and verifiers
- Schedule assurance engagements and manage timelines
- Address audit findings and coordinate remediation plans
- Maintain relationships with verification bodies (DNV, SGS, Deloitte)
- Ensure compliance with assurance standards (AA1000, ISAE 3000)

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Define reporting period, organizational scope, and geographic boundaries
- Identify primary stakeholder groups and their ESG expectations
- Select applicable reporting frameworks (GRI Standards, SASB, TCFD, SDGs)
- Audit existing data collection systems and quality gaps
- Review prior-year reports and stakeholder feedback

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Conduct materiality assessment and prioritize ESG topics
- Gather and consolidate environmental, social, governance data from operations
- Benchmark performance against industry peers and targets
- Draft narrative sections and integrate visual elements
- Conduct internal reviews with leadership and departmental stakeholders

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Finalize report layout, graphics, and formatting
- Arrange external assurance/verification engagement
- Incorporate audit comments and corrective actions
- Publish report and communicate through stakeholder channels
- Archive documentation and establish feedback mechanisms

## Deliverables
- Materiality Matrix (topics prioritized by significance and stakeholder concern)
- ESG Data Collection Template (standardized metrics by environmental/social/governance)
- Stakeholder Engagement Report (consultation process, outcomes, feedback summary)
- Integrated ESG Narrative Document (strategy, performance, targets, case studies)
- Assurance Statement (independent verification of selected metrics)
- Executive Summary and Digital Dashboard (key metrics, targets, progress visualization)
- Stakeholder Response Plan (addressing concerns, transparency roadmap)

## Extension Skills
- Carbon Accounting & GHG Protocol compliance
- Diversity & Inclusion metrics and reporting
- Supply chain sustainability and human rights due diligence

## Error Handling
- **Incomplete data**: Establish data collection timelines; use proxy metrics and estimates with clear disclosure in report
- **Framework mismatch**: Compare frameworks early; select primary framework and map secondary frameworks as supplementary
- **Stakeholder conflict**: Document competing priorities; present trade-offs transparently with rationale
- **Verification delays**: Schedule audits 2+ months before publication; prepare audit readiness documentation early
- **Greenwashing risk**: Ensure targets are science-based, ambitious, and aligned with transition plans; third-party assurance critical
- **Scope boundary disputes**: Clearly document scope decisions (Scope 1/2/3 emissions, direct/indirect impacts) upfront with stakeholder buy-in
