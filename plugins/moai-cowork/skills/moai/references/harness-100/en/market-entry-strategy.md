# Market Entry Strategy (harness-100-market)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
Market Entry Strategy is a structured framework for analyzing new geographic or product markets and selecting optimal entry modes (export, joint venture, subsidiary, licensing). This harness guides non-technical users through target market assessment, competitive analysis, regulatory landscape mapping, and go-to-market planning to minimize entry risk and maximize market penetration.

## Expert Roles

**Market Research & Analysis Lead**
- Conduct market sizing, growth trends, and demand forecasting
- Analyze customer segments, purchasing behaviors, and key needs
- Profile competitive landscape and competitive positioning
- Assess market attractiveness (size, growth, profitability, accessibility)
- Identify market gaps and differentiation opportunities

**Entry Mode Strategist**
- Evaluate export, agent/distributor, joint venture, and subsidiary options
- Assess capital requirements, speed-to-market, and control implications for each mode
- Model financial projections under different entry scenarios
- Identify barriers to entry and mitigation strategies
- Recommend optimal entry mode based on strategic objectives and constraints

**Regulatory & Compliance Navigator**
- Map regulatory requirements (licensing, certifications, standards, labeling)
- Identify product modification needs for local market compliance
- Assess intellectual property protection and enforcement capability
- Document import/export procedures and tariff implications
- Recommend compliance roadmap and timeline

**Go-to-Market Planning Manager**
- Develop market entry roadmap with phased approach and milestones
- Design distribution channel strategy (direct, partner, hybrid)
- Plan marketing and brand positioning for target market
- Establish pricing strategy relative to local competition and willingness-to-pay
- Identify key partnerships (distributors, agents, technology partners)

**Risk & Localization Specialist**
- Assess country-specific risks (political, economic, currency, regulatory)
- Develop contingency plans and mitigation strategies
- Design product/service localization (language, cultural adaptation, packaging)
- Evaluate supply chain resilience and local sourcing options
- Plan scenario analysis (best case, base case, downside case)

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Define strategic objectives and market entry timeline
- Identify candidate target markets based on opportunity and alignment
- Assess organizational capabilities and resource constraints
- Review prior market entry experience and lessons learned
- Establish decision criteria and success metrics

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Conduct market sizing and attractiveness analysis for target markets
- Perform competitive landscape analysis and market positioning assessment
- Map regulatory and compliance requirements by market
- Evaluate entry mode options with financial projections
- Develop detailed go-to-market plan with distribution, pricing, marketing

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Validate market research with customer interviews and industry experts
- Conduct leadership review and secure entry decision approval
- Establish implementation timeline and resource allocation
- Plan first-phase launch activities and early performance metrics
- Establish monitoring and adjustment procedures for ongoing market adaptation

## Deliverables
- Market Assessment Report (size, growth, trends, competitive analysis, attractiveness)
- Entry Mode Analysis (options, pros/cons, financial projections, recommendation)
- Regulatory Compliance Roadmap (requirements, timeline, certification/licensing needs)
- Go-to-Market Plan (distribution, pricing, marketing, partnership strategy)
- Risk Assessment & Mitigation Plan (country risks, contingency scenarios)
- Financial Projections (revenue, costs, profitability, payback period by entry mode)
- Implementation Timeline & Resource Plan (phased approach, milestones, resource allocation)

## Extension Skills
- Currency risk management and hedging strategies
- Supply chain localization and sourcing strategies
- Brand adaptation and marketing localization approaches

## Error Handling
- **Market size overestimation**: Validate sizing with multiple methodologies; be conservative in projections; test assumptions with customer research
- **Regulatory surprise**: Research regulations early; engage local counsel; build compliance timeline buffer; maintain contingency budget
- **Distribution partner conflicts**: Clearly document territory rights and exclusivity terms; establish performance metrics; include exit/termination clauses
- **Competitive response**: Monitor competitor actions; maintain pricing flexibility; build differentiation beyond initial product features
- **Currency fluctuation**: Establish pricing strategy considering forex risk; use currency hedging if material; review P&L impact regularly
- **Execution delay**: Break go-to-market into smaller milestones; maintain contingency timeline; prioritize quick-win initial activities
