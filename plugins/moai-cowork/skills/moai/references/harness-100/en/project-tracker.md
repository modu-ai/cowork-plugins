# Project Tracker (harness-103)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
The Project Tracker harness enables disciplined project management from planning through completion. It supports systematic project plan creation with clear objectives and scope definition, real-time milestone tracking with progress visualization, intelligent resource allocation balancing capacity and priorities, proactive risk log management identifying and mitigating threats, and professional status report generation communicating project health to stakeholders.

## Expert Roles

**Project Planning Director**
- Develops comprehensive project plans with clear objectives and deliverables
- Defines scope boundaries and manages scope creep
- Creates work breakdown structures with task hierarchies
- Establishes timelines, dependencies, and critical path analysis
- Documents assumptions, constraints, and success criteria

**Milestone & Progress Tracker**
- Establishes measurable milestone definitions and acceptance criteria
- Implements tracking systems and progress visualization methods
- Monitors schedule adherence and variance analysis
- Identifies delays early and recommends corrective actions
- Creates milestone dashboards for executive visibility

**Resource Allocation Specialist**
- Assesses team capacity, skills, and availability
- Assigns tasks matching skill requirements with workload balance
- Identifies resource conflicts and constraints
- Develops hiring or outsourcing strategies for gaps
- Optimizes resource utilization and prevents bottlenecks

**Risk Management Officer**
- Identifies project risks and potential threat scenarios
- Assesses risk probability, impact, and mitigation strategies
- Creates risk register with ownership and monitoring protocols
- Escalates emerging risks to appropriate stakeholders
- Conducts lessons learned reviews post-project

**Communications & Reporting Manager**
- Creates status reports with clear health indicators
- Translates technical details for executive audiences
- Develops stakeholder communication plans and cadences
- Generates variance analysis and trend reports
- Facilitates steering committee meetings and decision forums

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Collect project charter and business objectives
- Identify stakeholder groups and communication needs
- Document existing team structure and resource availability
- Clarify success metrics and acceptance criteria
- Understand organizational constraints and risks

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Create detailed project plan with work breakdown structure
- Establish milestone map with dependencies and critical path
- Build resource allocation plan with capacity analysis
- Develop risk register with mitigation strategies
- Design status report templates and reporting cadences

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Validate plan with stakeholders and resource providers
- Confirm milestone definitions are measurable and achievable
- Verify resource allocations are realistic and accepted
- Establish risk monitoring protocols and escalation triggers
- Create tracking dashboards and communication channels

## Deliverables
- Project Plan & WBS (scope, timeline, dependencies, critical path)
- Milestone Map & Tracking Dashboard (milestones, acceptance criteria, progress)
- Resource Allocation Plan (team assignments, capacity analysis, skill mapping)
- Risk Register & Mitigation Log (identified risks, probability/impact, mitigation strategies)
- Status Report Templates & Communication Plan (weekly/monthly reports, stakeholder cadences)

## Extension Skills
- **Agile/Scrum Master** — Adapts project tracker for iterative delivery with sprints and retrospectives
- **Budget & Cost Tracker** — Manages project financials alongside timeline and resource tracking
- **Stakeholder Engagement Specialist** — Develops strategies for managing expectations and securing commitment

## Error Handling

**Vague or Misaligned Objectives** — Facilitate objective definition workshop using SMART criteria. Request executive alignment before planning. Document rationale for scope decisions.

**Unrealistic Timeline Estimates** — Use historical data or benchmarks for validation. Build in contingency time for complexity. Recommend breaking large tasks into smaller, estimable chunks.

**Resource Overallocation** — Identify highest-priority work and defer lower-priority items. Recommend hiring, outsourcing, or phased approach. Create resource conflict resolution protocol.

**Emerging Risks Not Escalated** — Establish risk monitoring cadence and trigger protocols. Train team on risk identification and reporting. Review risk register in weekly standups.

**Poor Stakeholder Alignment** — Schedule alignment meetings with clear agendas and decisions. Create executive summary version of project plan. Establish sponsor decision authority upfront.

**Status Reports Not Actionable** — Focus reports on variance and decisions needed. Use traffic light status (red/yellow/green). Include specific recommendations for addressing red items.
