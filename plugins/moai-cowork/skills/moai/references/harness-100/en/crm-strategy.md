# CRM Strategy (harness-100-crm-strategy)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
The CRM Strategy harness enables sales teams, customer success managers, and business leaders to design and implement customer relationship management programs that maximize lifetime value and retention. From customer lifecycle mapping and segmentation strategies to loyalty program design and retention campaign planning, this harness provides frameworks for building deeper, more profitable customer relationships. Ideal for organizations looking to scale customer retention and personalization.

## Expert Roles
**1. Customer Lifecycle Manager**
Maps the complete customer journey from acquisition through advocacy. Responsibilities: design lifecycle stages, identify key touchpoints, define transition criteria, create stage-specific engagement strategies, track metrics at each phase.

**2. Segmentation Specialist**
Builds data-driven customer segments for targeted engagement. Responsibilities: analyze customer attributes and behaviors, define segment criteria, calculate segment size and value, develop segment-specific messaging, monitor segment performance.

**3. Retention Strategist**
Develops campaigns and programs to reduce churn and increase loyalty. Responsibilities: identify churn risk indicators, design win-back campaigns, create loyalty program structures, calculate ROI of retention initiatives, measure engagement impact.

**4. Customer Intelligence Analyst**
Extracts insights from customer data to drive strategy. Responsibilities: analyze purchase patterns, calculate customer lifetime value, identify growth opportunities, develop predictive churn models, create actionable dashboards.

**5. Program Architect**
Designs operational frameworks for CRM execution. Responsibilities: define CRM workflows and automation rules, establish governance frameworks, create communication cadences, design incentive structures, develop measurement dashboards.

## Workflow
### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Audit current customer database and data quality
- Identify business goals, revenue targets, and retention metrics
- Analyze historical customer data and churn patterns
- Define target customer persona and value characteristics

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Map customer lifecycle stages with specific behaviors
- Segment customers by attributes, value, and risk
- Design retention campaigns for high-risk segments
- Create loyalty program structure and incentive tiers
- Develop operational playbooks and automation rules

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Validate strategy against business objectives and constraints
- Calculate projected retention impact and ROI
- Prepare implementation roadmap and timelines
- Document performance metrics and KPI dashboards
- Create training materials for team adoption

## Deliverables
- **Lifecycle Map:** Visual stages with transition rules and engagement touchpoints
- **Segmentation Model:** Customer segments with size, value, and characteristic profiles
- **Retention Campaign Plan:** Campaigns by risk segment with messaging and cadence
- **Loyalty Program Design:** Tier structure, earning rules, rewards, and benefits
- **CRM Playbook:** Workflows, automation rules, and operational procedures

## Extension Skills
- **Churn Prediction:** Build models to identify at-risk customers before they leave
- **Personalization Engine:** Design dynamic, behavior-triggered customer communications
- **Win-Back Campaign:** Create reactivation strategies for churned customers

## Error Handling
| Error | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Low Segment Adoption | Unclear segment definitions | Simplify segment criteria, align with business language, validate with sales |
| Ineffective Retention | Misaligned incentives or messaging | Test messaging variants, adjust reward structure, increase communication frequency |
| Data Quality Issues | Incomplete or inaccurate customer records | Audit data sources, implement validation rules, establish data governance process |
| ROI Not Achieved | Unrealistic projections or poor execution | Reduce initial scope, focus on high-impact segments, improve team training |
