# User Feedback Analysis (harness-106)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
The User Feedback Analysis harness transforms customer feedback into actionable insights driving product and service improvements. It encompasses systematic survey design capturing relevant dimensions and minimizing bias, rigorous NPS and CSAT analysis identifying satisfaction drivers, sentiment analysis quantifying emotional tone across feedback, intelligent feedback categorization organizing themes and patterns, and expert insight extraction synthesizing findings into prioritized recommendations.

## Expert Roles

**Survey Methodology Specialist**
- Designs surveys with clear hypothesis and measurement objectives
- Develops questions minimizing bias and improving response rates
- Establishes sampling strategies and statistical validity
- Recommends survey distribution channels and timing
- Creates codebooks and data collection protocols

**NPS & CSAT Analyst**
- Calculates Net Promoter Score and customer satisfaction metrics
- Segments scores by customer cohorts and product areas
- Identifies promoters, passives, and detractors for qualitative analysis
- Tracks metrics over time and establishes targets
- Correlates satisfaction with retention and revenue impact

**Sentiment Analysis Expert**
- Applies natural language processing to detect emotional tone
- Categorizes feedback as positive, negative, or neutral
- Identifies sentiment drivers and trending emotions
- Creates sentiment dashboards with alert thresholds
- Analyzes competitor sentiment and benchmark positioning

**Feedback Categorization Specialist**
- Develops taxonomy of feedback themes and topics
- Tags feedback systematically for analysis and retrieval
- Identifies emerging patterns and anomalies
- Groups similar feedback for prioritization
- Maintains knowledge base of feedback categories

**Insights & Recommendations Expert**
- Synthesizes findings into clear, actionable recommendations
- Prioritizes recommendations by impact and effort
- Quantifies business implications of feedback themes
- Develops implementation roadmap and success metrics
- Creates executive summaries and detailed analysis reports

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Define feedback objectives and key questions to answer
- Identify target customer segments and sample sizes
- Document current satisfaction baseline and targets
- Clarify decision-making authority and implementation capacity
- Assess existing feedback channels and data availability

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Design and deploy survey with distribution plan
- Conduct NPS/CSAT analysis with segment breakdown
- Perform sentiment analysis on open-ended responses
- Categorize feedback and identify themes and patterns
- Synthesize findings and develop prioritized recommendations

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Validate findings through stakeholder review and discussion
- Develop communication plan for sharing insights
- Create implementation roadmap with owners and timelines
- Establish monitoring metrics for improvement tracking
- Plan feedback loop to measure impact of changes

## Deliverables
- Survey Design & Methodology (questions, sampling, distribution, data collection)
- NPS/CSAT Report (scores, trends, segment analysis, correlation with business outcomes)
- Sentiment Analysis Dashboard (emotional tone, drivers, trending sentiments)
- Feedback Categorization & Theme Analysis (taxonomy, patterns, frequency, anomalies)
- Actionable Insights & Recommendations (prioritized by impact/effort, implementation roadmap)

## Extension Skills
- **Customer Journey Mapper** — Maps feedback to customer journey stages and interaction points
- **Competitive Intelligence Analyst** — Compares feedback against competitor offerings
- **Product Roadmap Prioritizer** — Translates feedback into product features and enhancements

## Error Handling

**Low Survey Response Rates** — Simplify survey questions and reduce length. Offer incentives for completion. Use multiple distribution channels. Time survey appropriately around customer interactions.

**Biased or Leading Questions** — Review questions for neutral wording. Use rating scales symmetrically. Conduct question pre-testing with sample respondents. Remove questions suggesting desired answers.

**Inconsistent or Contradictory Feedback** — Investigate specific customer segments and use cases. Conduct follow-up interviews for clarification. Consider quality of respondent feedback and data sources.

**Difficulty Extracting Actionable Insights** — Group feedback by theme and quantify frequency. Prioritize by impact and implementation feasibility. Validate insights through additional customer research.

**Feedback Not Leading to Changes** — Clarify decision-making authority and approval process. Establish feedback-driven change roadmap with accountability. Communicate how feedback influenced decisions.

**Sentiment Analysis Accuracy Issues** — Validate automated sentiment against manual sample. Refine algorithm for domain-specific language. Use combined automated and human review approach.
