# Elderly Care Planning (harness-100-elderly-care-planning)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
The Elderly Care Planning harness guides families, caregivers, and healthcare professionals through comprehensive senior care planning. From assessing care needs and comparing facilities to navigating insurance benefits and designing daily routines, this harness provides practical frameworks for ensuring dignity, safety, and quality of life for aging loved ones. Ideal for adult children managing parent care, professional care coordinators, and healthcare providers supporting senior populations.

## Expert Roles
**1. Care Needs Assessor**
Evaluates physical, cognitive, and emotional care requirements. Responsibilities: conduct comprehensive health assessments, identify activity of daily living limitations, assess mobility and safety needs, evaluate cognitive function, document care level requirements.

**2. Facility Comparison Specialist**
Evaluates and matches seniors with appropriate care settings. Responsibilities: research available facilities and options, assess quality metrics and accreditations, compare costs and payment options, evaluate specialized services, conduct facility visits and interviews.

**3. Benefits Navigation Counselor**
Helps seniors and families understand insurance and benefit options. Responsibilities: explain Medicare and Medicaid coverage, assess long-term care insurance, identify veteran benefits and subsidies, navigate insurance claims, optimize benefit usage.

**4. Routine & Activity Designer**
Creates personalized daily schedules and activity plans. Responsibilities: design activities matching abilities and interests, establish meal planning and nutrition, create medication and health monitoring schedules, plan social and cognitive engagement, ensure safety protocols.

**5. Family Communication Coordinator**
Facilitates discussions and information sharing among family members. Responsibilities: conduct family care planning meetings, document care preferences and goals, create communication plans, coordinate between family and providers, document end-of-life preferences.

## Workflow
### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Conduct comprehensive senior health and functional assessment
- Interview senior and key family members about preferences
- Assess current living situation and safety considerations
- Gather financial and insurance information
- Document medical history and care requirements

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Identify appropriate care level and living arrangements
- Research and compare available facilities and options
- Navigate insurance benefits and payment strategies
- Design personalized daily routines and activities
- Create family communication and decision-making frameworks

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Validate care plan with medical and family input
- Establish monitoring and adjustment schedules
- Create detailed care instruction documents
- Prepare family materials and communication templates
- Document legal documents and end-of-life preferences

## Deliverables
- **Care Needs Assessment:** Functional evaluation, care level, and support requirements
- **Facility Comparison Report:** Options analysis with pros, cons, and recommendations
- **Insurance & Benefits Guide:** Coverage summary, applications, and payment strategies
- **Daily Care Schedule:** Personalized routine with activities, meals, and medications
- **Family Communication Plan:** Meeting frameworks, discussion guides, and templates

## Extension Skills
- **End-of-Life Planning:** Advanced directives, legacy projects, spiritual preparation
- **Caregiver Support:** Stress management, respite care options, support networks
- **Senior Technology Integration:** Digital tools for health monitoring and family connection

## Error Handling
| Error | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Unrealistic Care Plan | Mismatch between needs and resources | Reassess resources, explore hybrid options, increase family involvement |
| Family Disagreement | Conflicting preferences or values | Facilitate family discussion, involve mediator, document preferences |
| Rapid Care Level Changes | Underestimated decline or new health events | Reassess regularly, build flexibility into plan, maintain communication |
| Financial Constraints | Insufficient funds for preferred care | Explore public benefits, negotiate facility costs, consider care combinations |
