# Invoice Management (harness-100-invoices)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
Invoice Management is a comprehensive framework for creating, tracking, and optimizing accounts receivable to improve cash flow and payment reliability. This harness guides non-technical users through invoice template creation, payment term negotiation, overdue follow-up procedures, and cash flow forecasting without requiring accounting software expertise.

## Expert Roles

**Invoice Design & Automation Manager**
- Create standardized invoice templates with all legally required elements
- Establish invoice numbering sequences and archival procedures
- Automate invoice generation from orders or service delivery records
- Define payment instructions, banking details, and payment method options
- Implement version control and audit trails for invoice modifications

**Payment Terms Optimizer**
- Assess customer credit profiles and payment history
- Negotiate payment terms (net 30, net 60, net 90) with strategic customers
- Design early payment discounts (e.g., 2/10 net 30) to accelerate collections
- Document terms in customer contracts and master agreements
- Monitor term compliance and flag unusual patterns

**Accounts Receivable Specialist**
- Track invoice issuance, delivery, and customer acknowledgment
- Maintain aging reports (current, 30/60/90+ days overdue)
- Monitor cash collection patterns and identify trends
- Reconcile payments against invoices and resolve discrepancies
- Generate weekly/monthly AR status reports for finance team

**Collections & Follow-Up Lead**
- Design escalating follow-up procedures (email, phone, formal letters)
- Track overdue accounts and coordinate with sales teams on payment recovery
- Negotiate payment plans for disputed or past-due invoices
- Document collection activities and maintain communication records
- Evaluate write-offs and bad debt reserve adequacy

**Cash Flow & Forecasting Analyst**
- Project cash inflow timing based on AR aging and customer payment patterns
- Model impact of payment term changes on cash position
- Identify seasonal collection patterns and adjust cash forecasts
- Calculate days sales outstanding (DSO) and benchmark against industry
- Recommend AR optimization strategies to improve liquidity

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Document current invoice process, payment terms, and collection procedures
- Assess customer credit profiles and historical payment patterns
- Analyze AR aging and identify overdue concentration by customer
- Review current payment methods (ACH, check, credit card, wire) and costs
- Benchmark payment terms and DSO against industry standards

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Design invoice templates with all required legal and business information
- Create payment term negotiation strategy by customer segment
- Develop AR tracking dashboard and aging report procedures
- Design collection escalation workflow (automated reminders, phone calls, letters)
- Build cash flow forecast model incorporating AR timing

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Implement invoice template and establish distribution procedures
- Launch automated payment reminders and collection workflows
- Monitor collections results and refine follow-up strategies
- Generate and review monthly AR aging and cash flow reports
- Adjust terms and procedures based on collection performance

## Deliverables
- Invoice Template (legally compliant, branded, all required fields)
- Payment Terms Matrix (customer-specific terms, early payment incentives)
- AR Aging Report (current, 30/60/90+ days, total outstanding by customer)
- Collections Escalation Procedure (email templates, phone scripts, letter templates)
- Cash Flow Forecast Model (projected inflows based on AR timing, DSO)
- Payment Methods Analysis (cost per method, customer preferences, optimization)
- DSO Benchmark Report (current DSO, industry comparison, improvement opportunities)

## Extension Skills
- Dunning and payment retry management for recurring invoicing
- Multi-currency invoicing and foreign exchange reconciliation
- Subscription and contract-based recurring billing models

## Error Handling
- **Invoice disputes**: Establish clear dispute resolution procedure; track disputed amounts separately; document supporting evidence
- **Incorrect payment allocation**: Implement manual reconciliation review; clarify customer payment references; follow up on ambiguous remittances
- **Cash flow variance**: Use rolling forecast with variance analysis; update payment pattern assumptions as actual data becomes available
- **Customer insolvency**: Establish early warning indicators (payment delays, communication silence); evaluate credit rating changes; consider factoring
- **Non-payment despite follow-up**: Escalate to formal demand letter; evaluate legal collection or litigation; recognize bad debt reserve
- **Payment processing delays**: Provide multiple payment methods; specify processing timelines; clarify when payment is "received" vs "cleared"
