# Brand Voice Guide (harness-brand-voice-guide)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
Brand Voice Guide helps marketing teams, content creators, and communicators develop and maintain a distinctive, consistent brand personality across all customer touchpoints. This harness defines tone-of-voice guidelines, establishes messaging frameworks, creates style guides, and ensures cross-channel consistency that strengthens brand recognition and customer trust.

## Expert Roles

- **Brand Personality Architect**: Shapes foundational brand character and emotional positioning
  - **Personality Mapping**: Defines core attributes (trustworthy, playful, bold, sophisticated) with supporting evidence
  - **Target Audience Alignment**: Ensures personality resonates with customer values, aspirations, and communication preferences
  - **Competitor Differentiation**: Distinguishes brand voice from competitor personas to create unique market position
  - **Evolution Planning**: Establishes how brand personality adapts across customer lifecycle and market conditions
  - **Visual-Verbal Sync**: Aligns brand voice with design language, color palette, and imagery style

- **Tone-of-Voice Master**: Translates brand personality into consistent written and spoken communication guidelines
  - **Voice Attribute Definition**: Operationalizes personality through specific voice characteristics (formal/informal, direct/indirect, technical/accessible)
  - **Tone Variation Framework**: Specifies how tone shifts across contexts (customer service, crisis communication, educational content, promotional)
  - **Do's and Don'ts Library**: Creates concrete examples of voice in action and common pitfalls to avoid
  - **Accessibility Standards**: Ensures tone is inclusive for diverse reading levels, cognitive abilities, and non-native speakers
  - **Emotional Resonance Testing**: Validates that tone evokes intended emotions (confidence, belonging, excitement)

- **Messaging Framework Designer**: Structures core narratives and key messages for consistent communication
  - **Brand Story Architecture**: Develops hero's journey narrative showing customer transformation and brand role
  - **Key Message Pillars**: Identifies 3-5 core messages that differentiate value proposition across stakeholder groups
  - **Audience-Specific Messaging**: Tailors messaging depth and emphasis for executives, practitioners, and end-users
  - **Competitive Positioning**: Articulates clear differentiation on capability, value, or values relative to alternatives
  - **Message Hierarchy**: Prioritizes messages by importance; specifies which combinations address specific objections

- **Style Guide Compiler**: Creates comprehensive reference documents ensuring consistent execution across channels and teams
  - **Grammar & Syntax Standards**: Specifies conventions (capitalization, punctuation, abbreviations, jargon usage)
  - **Word Choice Library**: Documents preferred terms, avoiding alternatives and deprecated terminology
  - **Visual Text Standards**: Establishes rules for headlines, subheadings, body copy, and calls-to-action
  - **Channel-Specific Adaptations**: Documents how core voice adapts to channel constraints (Twitter character limits, email tone, voicemail)
  - **Template Gallery**: Provides pre-written templates for common communication needs (welcome emails, error messages, announcements)

- **Cross-Channel Consistency Manager**: Oversees brand voice deployment across web, social, email, video, and sales
  - **Channel Audit**: Assesses current voice consistency across all customer touchpoints and identifies deviations
  - **Rollout Planning**: Sequences style guide adoption across channels with training for content creators
  - **Quality Review Standards**: Defines review process, approval authorities, and escalation paths for voice violations
  - **Evolution Tracking**: Monitors brand voice performance, customer sentiment, and competitive shifts over time
  - **Feedback Loop**: Collects content creator feedback and customer perception data to refine guidelines

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
1. **Stakeholder Interviews**: Document leadership vision, customer insights, and brand heritage from key stakeholders
2. **Competitive Audit**: Analyze competitor voice, market positioning, and gaps in emotional space
3. **Customer Feedback**: Conduct surveys and focus groups on how customers perceive current brand voice and personality
4. **Channel Assessment**: Document existing communication across web, social, email, and sales to identify inconsistencies
5. **Content Inventory**: Audit current messaging themes, key phrases, and communication patterns

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production

| Order | Task | Owner | Depends On | Deliverable |
|-------|------|-------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | Define brand personality attributes | Personality Architect | Stakeholder Input | `brand-personality-profile.md` |
| 2 | Establish tone-of-voice guidelines | Tone Master | Personality Profile | `tone-of-voice-guide.md` |
| 3 | Develop messaging frameworks | Message Designer | Personality Profile | `key-messages-framework.md` |
| 4 | Create comprehensive style guide | Style Compiler | Tone Guide + Messaging | `brand-voice-style-guide.md` |
| 5 | Audit current channel voice | Consistency Manager | Style Guide | `channel-voice-audit.md` |
| 6 | Design rollout and training | Consistency Manager | Channel Audit | `implementation-plan.md` |
| 7 | Compile reference templates | Style Compiler | All Above | `voice-templates-library.md` |

**Inter-agent Communication**: Personality Architect briefs Tone Master on core attributes; Message Designer aligns narrative with Personality definition; Consistency Manager flags implementation barriers with Tone Master; Style Compiler integrates all guidelines into unified reference.

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
1. **Leadership Sign-off**: Present brand personality and voice to executive team for strategic validation
2. **Cross-functional Review**: Share style guide with marketing, sales, customer service, and product teams for feasibility feedback
3. **Pilot Testing**: Deploy voice across select channels and measure customer response before full rollout
4. **Team Training**: Conduct workshops with content creators on brand voice application and quality standards
5. **Monitoring Setup**: Establish metrics, review processes, and feedback mechanisms for ongoing voice consistency

## Deliverables
- **brand-personality-profile.md**: Brand attributes, values, customer archetype, and competitive differentiation
- **tone-of-voice-guide.md**: Voice characteristics, tone variations by context, do's/don'ts, and accessibility standards
- **key-messages-framework.md**: Brand story, message pillars, audience-specific messaging, and objection handling
- **brand-voice-style-guide.md**: Comprehensive reference with grammar, word choice, formatting, and channel adaptations
- **channel-voice-audit.md**: Assessment of current voice consistency with specific recommendations by channel
- **implementation-plan.md**: Rollout timeline, team training approach, quality review standards, and success metrics
- **voice-templates-library.md**: Pre-written templates for common communications aligned to brand voice

## Extension Skills
- **Visual Brand Sync**: Ensures written brand voice aligns with visual identity, imagery, and design language
- **Crisis Communication Playbook**: Adapts brand voice for sensitive situations while maintaining authenticity
- **Multilingual Voice Adaptation**: Guides voice translation across languages while preserving personality and emotional resonance

## Error Handling

| Error Type | Strategy |
|-----------|----------|
| Brand Voice Too Narrow | Expand flexibility guidelines to allow personality expression while maintaining consistency |
| Inconsistent Channel Execution | Strengthen review process; provide more granular templates for unique channel constraints |
| Voice Misalignment with Target | Test revised voice with customer samples; conduct focus groups before full rollout |
| Tone Perceived as Inauthentic | Simplify guidelines; anchor to real customer stories; involve sales/service teams in messaging development |
| Competitive Voice Convergence | Sharpen differentiation; explore underutilized emotional dimensions; periodically refresh brand voice |
| Global/Local Tension | Define core voice principles; allow regional adaptation on tone, examples, and cultural references |
