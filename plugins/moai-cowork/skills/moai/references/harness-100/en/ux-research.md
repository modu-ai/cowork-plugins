# UX Research (harness-107)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
The UX Research harness enables systematic investigation of user needs, behaviors, and pain points to inform product design and strategy. It supports user interview guide creation capturing deep behavioral insights, usability test planning and execution identifying friction points, comprehensive persona development enabling user-centered decision making, detailed journey mapping revealing touchpoints and opportunities, and research findings synthesis translating discoveries into strategic recommendations.

## Expert Roles

**User Interview Guide Designer**
- Develops semi-structured interview guides exploring user motivations and behaviors
- Designs questions minimizing leading bias while exploring deeply
- Creates interview protocols and research templates
- Recommends participant selection and recruitment strategies
- Develops analysis frameworks for qualitative data synthesis

**Usability Testing Coordinator**
- Designs usability test protocols and task scenarios
- Develops testing infrastructure and observation procedures
- Recruits and screens test participants matching target profiles
- Facilitates testing sessions and captures behavioral data
- Analyzes findings and prioritizes usability issues

**Persona Development Specialist**
- Synthesizes user research into detailed personas
- Documents persona attributes, goals, pain points, and behaviors
- Creates persona empathy maps and value propositions
- Develops use case scenarios for each persona
- Establishes persona governance and update processes

**Journey Mapping Expert**
- Creates comprehensive customer journey maps with all touchpoints
- Identifies moments of truth and critical interaction points
- Maps user emotions, pain points, and opportunities
- Documents systems, processes, and stakeholders involved
- Develops journey recommendations and improvement priorities

**Research Insights & Strategy Officer**
- Synthesizes research findings into clear, strategic insights
- Identifies patterns and themes across multiple research methods
- Develops prioritized recommendations with business rationale
- Creates actionable design requirements from research
- Translates insights into product roadmap and strategy

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Clarify research objectives and key questions to answer
- Define target users and participant profiles
- Document existing product knowledge and assumptions
- Identify stakeholders and decision-making authority
- Assess timeline and resource constraints

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Create and refine interview guides and test protocols
- Conduct user interviews and usability testing sessions
- Analyze qualitative findings and identify patterns
- Develop personas based on research data
- Create journey maps with detailed touchpoint analysis

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Present findings to stakeholders with supporting evidence
- Develop prioritized recommendations with impact assessment
- Create design requirements and specifications from research
- Establish implementation roadmap and success metrics
- Plan ongoing research to validate and refine insights

## Deliverables
- User Interview Guide (questions, protocols, participant criteria, analysis framework)
- Usability Test Plan & Results (task scenarios, findings, issue prioritization, recommendations)
- User Personas (demographics, goals, pain points, behaviors, use cases for 3-5 key personas)
- Journey Maps (touchpoints, emotions, opportunities, pain points across user lifecycle)
- Research Synthesis Report (key insights, strategic recommendations, implementation priorities)

## Extension Skills
- **Diary Study Designer** — Creates longitudinal research capturing user behaviors over time
- **Accessibility Research Specialist** — Evaluates product usability for users with disabilities
- **Quantitative UX Researcher** — Designs surveys and analytics to validate qualitative findings

## Error Handling

**Difficulty Recruiting Representative Participants** — Use multiple recruitment channels (user databases, social media, partner networks). Offer incentives for participation. Relax some criteria if necessary while maintaining core target profile.

**Participants Not Revealing True Behaviors** — Build rapport and psychological safety. Use observational tasks versus self-report. Conduct follow-up interviews for clarification. Look for incongruence between stated and observed behaviors.

**Unclear or Conflicting Findings** — Conduct additional interviews or testing to clarify. Segment findings by user type or context. Present conflicting perspectives honestly with interpretations.

**Personas Not Adopted by Product Teams** — Involve product team in persona development process. Create simplified persona one-pagers for easy reference. Use personas in design decision reviews and roadmap discussions.

**Research Insights Not Translating to Action** — Present findings with clear business implications and quantified impact. Create design specifications and feature requirements from research. Establish feedback loop showing design changes implemented from research.

**Limited Resources for Comprehensive Research** — Prioritize most critical research questions. Use rapid research methods (unmoderated testing, online surveys). Plan research in phases aligned with product development cycles.
