# Import/Export (harness-100-trade)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
Import/Export is a structured reference for managing international trade transactions, compliance documentation, and logistics coordination. This harness guides non-technical users through customs procedures, tariff classification, trade agreement navigation, and trade finance mechanisms to ensure smooth, compliant cross-border commerce without requiring deep customs expertise.

## Expert Roles

**Trade Compliance Officer**
- Verify product HS code classification and tariff implications
- Ensure all export documentation meets destination country regulations
- Monitor sanctions lists and prohibited goods restrictions
- Prepare customs declarations and compliance certificates
- Maintain audit trails and regulatory correspondence

**Customs Documentation Specialist**
- Create accurate commercial invoices, packing lists, and certificates of origin
- Prepare bill of lading and shipping documents
- Manage product description and classification for customs purposes
- Coordinate with customs brokers and border authorities
- Ensure data accuracy and consistency across all documentation

**Trade Finance Coordinator**
- Structure letter of credit (L/C) agreements with banks and trading partners
- Coordinate payment terms (cash, documentary credit, open account)
- Manage trade insurance policies for shipment protection
- Track payment obligations and settlement procedures
- Negotiate financing terms that balance risk and cost

**Incoterms & Logistics Manager**
- Select appropriate Incoterms (FOB, CIF, DDP, DAP) based on business needs
- Define shipping responsibilities, insurance, and risk allocation
- Coordinate freight forwarding and carrier selection
- Plan shipping routes considering tariffs and transit times
- Manage logistics documentation and tracking

**Market Entry Advisor**
- Assess tariff rates and preferential trade agreements (FTA, GSP)
- Identify regulatory requirements in target markets
- Evaluate landed cost implications including all duties and taxes
- Recommend entry strategies (direct export, agency, partnership)
- Provide market-specific compliance roadmaps

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Define product specifications, origin, and destination market
- Research applicable tariff rates, quota restrictions, and trade agreements
- Identify customer requirements and payment preferences
- Assess logistics capabilities and carrier options
- Review currency and payment term constraints

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Classify products using HS nomenclature and verify tariff classification
- Prepare commercial documentation (invoice, packing list, COO)
- Calculate landed costs including tariffs, taxes, insurance, freight
- Structure payment terms (L/C, open account, cash) with risk mitigation
- Coordinate with customs brokers and freight forwarders

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Finalize all export documentation and cross-check accuracy
- Arrange customs clearance and pre-shipment inspection if required
- Confirm payment mechanisms and settlement procedures
- Track shipment and resolve customs delays or documentation issues
- Archive all documentation for audit and compliance record-keeping

## Deliverables
- HS Code Classification Report (product classification, tariff rate, preference eligibility)
- Commercial Invoice and Packing List (standardized templates with required fields)
- Certificate of Origin (for preferential trade agreement qualification)
- Customs Declaration Form (country-specific, pre-filled with product details)
- Letter of Credit Agreement (terms, conditions, payment triggers)
- Landed Cost Analysis (tariffs, taxes, freight, insurance, total cost-to-customer)
- Trade Compliance Checklist (destination-specific regulatory requirements)

## Extension Skills
- Tariff rate quotas and import licensing procedures
- Sanction screening and export control compliance
- Inbound logistics and customs clearance for imports

## Error Handling
- **Incorrect HS classification**: Use tariff authorities' databases and prior rulings; consult customs brokers if classification uncertain
- **Missing or incorrect documents**: Maintain template library; implement document checklist before shipment
- **Currency fluctuation risk**: Lock exchange rates in contracts; use forward contracts or currency hedging
- **Customs delays**: Prepare documentation in advance; communicate proactively with brokers; understand clearance timelines by route
- **Trade agreement misapplication**: Verify origin criteria and preference eligibility; maintain audit trail of materials sourcing
- **Payment default risk**: Structure L/C terms; negotiate payment security with financial institutions; consider trade credit insurance
