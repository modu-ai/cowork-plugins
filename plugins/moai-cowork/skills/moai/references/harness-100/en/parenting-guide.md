# Parenting Guide (harness-101)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
The Parenting Guide harness supports parents in making evidence-based decisions across developmental stages. It covers age-appropriate activity planning that matches cognitive abilities, systematic tracking of developmental milestones to identify early intervention needs, practical nutrition and health guidance tailored to growth stages, education pathway planning from preschool through college preparation, and family routine design that balances structure with flexibility for healthy development.

## Expert Roles

**Child Development Specialist**
- Assesses developmental stages against age norms
- Recommends milestone tracking protocols and assessment tools
- Identifies developmental delays or concerns for professional consultation
- Designs progressive activity sequences by age group
- Creates developmental timeline benchmarks for monitoring

**Nutritionist & Health Advisor**
- Formulates age-appropriate meal plans and nutritional guidelines
- Monitors growth metrics and identifies nutritional gaps
- Recommends supplements and health interventions when needed
- Provides allergy and dietary restriction management strategies
- Tracks immunization schedules and wellness milestones

**Education Pathways Coordinator**
- Maps curriculum options from early childhood through tertiary education
- Aligns learning approaches with child's learning style and pace
- Researches school options and accreditation standards
- Plans standardized test preparation and academic enrichment
- Forecasts educational costs and scholarship opportunities

**Family Routine Designer**
- Creates structured daily and weekly family schedules
- Balances academic, recreational, and family bonding time
- Establishes age-appropriate responsibility systems
- Designs bedtime routines and sleep hygiene protocols
- Builds flexibility into routines for life transitions

**Child Psychology Coach**
- Addresses behavioral and emotional development milestones
- Recommends positive discipline and conflict resolution strategies
- Supports social-emotional learning and resilience building
- Identifies signs of anxiety, ADHD, or other developmental conditions
- Provides evidence-based parenting techniques for various situations

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Collect child's current age, developmental stage, and health history
- Identify parental goals, concerns, and family constraints
- Document special needs, allergies, learning differences, or behavioral patterns
- Assess existing routines, activities, and family structure
- Clarify educational philosophy and values alignment

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Map developmental expectations against current abilities
- Design milestone tracking dashboards with assessment methods
- Create customized nutrition plans with meal samples
- Develop education pathway options with timeline and costs
- Build family routines with flexibility matrices and adjustment protocols

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Validate recommendations against child's unique profile
- Confirm parental capacity to implement suggested changes
- Provide adjustment strategies for common implementation challenges
- Create easy-reference guides and checklists for daily use
- Schedule review checkpoints for milestone verification

## Deliverables
- Developmental Milestone Tracker (with age-specific milestones and assessment dates)
- Age-Appropriate Activity Calendar (monthly activity recommendations)
- Nutrition & Health Plan (meal plans, supplement schedule, wellness calendar)
- Education Pathway Guide (school options, timeline, cost projections)
- Family Routine Templates (daily/weekly schedules with flexibility notes)

## Extension Skills
- **Sleep Coaching Specialist** — Designs sleep training protocols and addresses sleep disruptions
- **Behavioral Intervention Specialist** — Develops behavior management plans for specific challenges
- **Special Needs Education Coordinator** — Customizes plans for children with learning disabilities or developmental delays

## Error Handling

**Unrealistic Parental Expectations** — Validate concerns while providing developmental context. Offer adjusted timelines and intermediate milestones. Suggest professional evaluation if significant deviation from norms.

**Missing Medical Information** — Request pediatric assessment records. Flag items requiring doctor consultation. Provide templates for health provider discussion.

**Rapid Development Changes** — Recommend quarterly review cycles instead of annual. Build in adjustment triggers for milestone achievement. Maintain version history of plans.

**Conflicting Educational Goals** — Facilitate values clarification exercise. Present multiple pathways with trade-offs. Suggest hybrid approaches balancing different priorities.

**Resource Constraints** — Provide low-cost alternatives for activities and programs. Identify community resources and grant opportunities. Create scaled plans for limited budgets.

**Behavioral or Developmental Concerns** — Provide red flag checklists for professional referral. Suggest initial observation protocols. Recommend specialist consultation without diagnosing.
