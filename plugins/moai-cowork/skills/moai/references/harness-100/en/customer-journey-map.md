# Customer Journey Map (harness-100-customer-journey-map)

> MoAI-Cowork V.0.1.3 Harness Reference

## Overview
The Customer Journey Map harness guides teams through creating detailed visualizations of how customers interact with your brand across all touchpoints. From awareness to advocacy, this harness helps identify pain points, moments of truth, and opportunities for improvement. Perfect for product teams, UX designers, customer success managers, and strategists who need to understand and optimize the complete customer experience at every stage.

## Expert Roles
**1. Journey Architect**
Designs the overall structure and flow of the customer experience. Responsibilities: define journey stages and substages, map primary and secondary paths, identify key decision points, establish timeline and duration expectations, document assumptions and dependencies.

**2. Touchpoint Researcher**
Investigates and catalogs every customer interaction point. Responsibilities: conduct customer interviews and research, identify all interaction channels, analyze touchpoint effectiveness, document customer actions at each point, assess technology and human touchpoints.

**3. Persona Journey Specialist**
Creates persona-specific journey variations for different customer segments. Responsibilities: develop detailed customer personas, map journey variations by persona, identify persona-specific pain points, design persona-based content and messaging, validate with target users.

**4. Pain Point Analyst**
Systematically uncovers friction and frustration in the journey. Responsibilities: identify emotional low points and barriers, prioritize pain points by impact and frequency, root-cause analysis of friction, recommend solution approaches, measure pain point improvement over time.

**5. Experience Designer**
Envisions and designs improved experience solutions. Responsibilities: design moment-of-truth experiences, recommend service improvements, prototype experience enhancements, validate designs with users, create implementation roadmaps.

## Workflow
### Phase 1: Context Gathering
- Conduct customer interviews and observation sessions
- Map current customer behaviors and touchpoints
- Identify relevant customer personas and segments
- Gather analytics data on customer interactions
- Document organizational constraints and capabilities

### Phase 2: Analysis & Production
- Create detailed journey maps by persona
- Identify emotions and sentiment at each stage
- Highlight pain points and moments of truth
- Analyze competitor or alternative journey experiences
- Design improved experience recommendations

### Phase 3: Review & Delivery
- Validate journey maps with actual customers
- Prioritize improvement opportunities by impact
- Create visual journey map artifacts and documentation
- Develop implementation roadmap with dependencies
- Establish KPIs for measuring journey improvements

## Deliverables
- **Journey Map Visualization:** Stage-by-stage map showing customer actions, emotions, and touchpoints
- **Persona Journey Profiles:** Journey variations for each primary customer segment
- **Pain Point Report:** Documented frustrations with root causes and priority rankings
- **Moment-of-Truth Analysis:** Critical experiences and their impact on customer decisions
- **Experience Improvement Roadmap:** Prioritized solution recommendations with timelines

## Extension Skills
- **Service Blueprint:** Design backend operations to support customer experience moments
- **Competitive Journey Analysis:** Map competitor or alternative user experiences for benchmarking
- **Emotional Journey Mapping:** Deeper analysis of customer emotions and psychological states

## Error Handling
| Error | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| Incomplete Journey | Missed customer research or data | Conduct additional interviews, analyze analytics, validate with real users |
| Persona Misalignment | Generic or inaccurate personas | Refine personas with deeper research, validate with customer segments |
| Over-Complicated Map | Too many stages or variations | Consolidate stages, focus on primary journey, create separate detailed maps |
| Low Adoption/Impact | Unclear recommendations or unrealistic scope | Prioritize high-impact improvements, simplify recommendations, pilot with small team |
